research

Writings, datasets, and code on international adjudication, judicial reasoning, and computational law.

A particular interest in the authority and effectiveness of international law, and the empirical study of how it is produced, used, and undergoes change.

Research has appeared in leading peer-reviewed journals and with major university presses. Tools and datasets for large-scale legal analysis — from OCR pipelines for court archives and citation mining across judicial decisions, to AI-powered legal research systems and comprehensive corpora of international legal materials — are released as companion materials. A comprehensive list is also available on Google Scholar. Inquiries from prospective PhD students with innovative projects are welcome.
