Professional Profile

I am a Senior Lecturer (Associate Professor) in Public International Law at The Dickson Poon School of Law, King’s College London. In addition, I serve as the Associate Director of the Centre for International Governance and Dispute Resolution (CIGAD).

My scholarship investigates the judicial reasoning and authority of international courts and tribunals. I am a proponent of applying empirical and computational methodologies to analyse legal argumentation and judicial behaviour, contributing a quantitative dimension to traditional legal scholarship.

Core Expertise

Public International Law

In-depth knowledge of foundational principles, including sources of law, state responsibility, human rights, and the law of the sea.

International Dispute Resolution

Extensive experience with the procedural and substantive practice of international courts and tribunals, including the ICJ and ICSID.

Computational Legal Studies

Pioneering the use of data science, including NLP and network analysis, to model and understand the dynamics of international law.

Practice & Consultancies

My academic work is complemented by an active legal practice. This dual focus ensures my scholarship is grounded in practical realities, and my practice is informed by rigorous scholarly inquiry. My experience includes, inter alia:

  • Serving as counsel and advocate for States before the International Court of Justice.
  • Acting as an assistant to arbitrators in investment treaty disputes administered by ICSID.
  • Providing specialist advice as a legal adviser to States on complex matters of public international law.

Research & Publications

My research on international adjudication, judicial reasoning, and computational law has been published in leading peer-reviewed journals and by major university presses. My work contributes to the theoretical and empirical understanding of how international courts operate and establish authority.

A comprehensive list of publications is available on my Google Scholar profile. I also welcome inquiries from prospective PhD students with innovative projects in my areas of expertise.
