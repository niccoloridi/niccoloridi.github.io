About Me

I am a Senior Lecturer (Associate Professor) in Public International Law at The Dickson Poon School of Law, King’s College London, where I also serve as the Associate Director of the Centre for International Governance and Dispute Resolution (CIGAD).

My academic and professional life is dedicated to the intersection of law, data, and global justice. My research explores the reasoning and authority of international courts and tribunals, with a particular focus on precedent, comity, and the evolving sources of international law. I am a pioneer in applying empirical and computational methods—including natural language processing and network analysis—to analyze legal texts and judicial behaviour.

Beyond academia, I actively engage in legal practice as counsel and advocate before the International Court of Justice (ICJ) and as an assistant to arbitral tribunals in ICSID cases. This practical experience directly informs my research and teaching, creating a dynamic feedback loop between theory and real-world application.

Core Expertise

Public International Law

Specializing in the law of state responsibility, sources of international law, human rights, and the law of the sea. My work examines the fundamental principles governing relations between states and international actors.

International Dispute Resolution

Extensive experience in the procedure and practice of international courts and tribunals, including the ICJ, investment arbitration (ICSID), and the WTO. My research analyzes judicial decision-making and the finality of judgments.

Law, AI & Computational Methods

A leading voice in digital legal studies. I develop open-source code and datasets to analyze legal documents, map scholarly influence, and explore the impact of generative AI on the legal profession and dispute settlement.
