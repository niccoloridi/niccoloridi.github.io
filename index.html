About Me

I am a Senior Lecturer (Associate Professor) in Public International Law at The Dickson Poon School of Law, King’s College London, where I also serve as the Associate Director of the Centre for International Governance and Dispute Resolution (CIGAD).

My research explores the reasoning and authority of international courts and tribunals, with a particular focus on precedent and the evolving sources of international law. I am a pioneer in applying empirical and computational methods—including natural language processing and network analysis—to analyze legal texts and judicial behaviour.

Beyond academia, I actively engage in legal practice as counsel and advocate before international courts and as an assistant to arbitral tribunals. This practical experience directly informs my research and teaching.

Core Expertise

Public International Law

Specializing in the law of state responsibility, sources of international law, human rights, and the law of the sea.

International Dispute Resolution

Extensive experience in the procedure and practice of international courts and tribunals, including the ICJ and investment arbitration (ICSID).

Law, AI & Computational Methods

Developing open-source tools to analyze legal documents and explore the impact of generative AI on the legal profession and dispute settlement.

Research & Publications

My research on international adjudication, precedent, and computational law has been published in leading international law journals. My work contributes to the theoretical and empirical understanding of how international courts operate and create authority.

For a comprehensive list of my work, please visit my Google Scholar profile.

Practice & Counsel

My academic work is complemented by an active role in legal practice. My experience includes:

  • Serving as counsel and advocate for States before the International Court of Justice.
  • Acting as an assistant to arbitrators in investment treaty disputes administered by ICSID.
  • Providing specialist advice as a legal adviser to States on complex matters of public international law.
  • Serving as a member of the Panel of Arbitrators for a Latin American arbitration centre.

Teaching & Supervision

I convene and teach modules at King's College London at both the undergraduate and postgraduate levels, including:

  • Generative AI and the Law
  • International Dispute Resolution
  • Public International Law
  • International Investment Law
